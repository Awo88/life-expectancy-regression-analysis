📋 Overview

This project uses regression analysis to model life expectancy across countries using World Health Organization data. By examining the relationship between life expectancy and key indicators — GDP, healthcare access, education, and disease prevalence — the model identifies which factors most strongly predict population health outcomes.

Built in R and applied to a global dataset spanning 190+ countries, the analysis produces interpretable coefficient estimates and out-of-sample forecasts that reveal the underlying drivers of longevity at a country level.

⚙ Methodology
01
Data Preparation
Clean and merge WHO datasets across countries and years. Handle missing values, outliers, and encode categorical variables.
02
Exploratory Analysis
Visualize distributions, correlations, and geographic patterns in life expectancy. Identify potential multicollinearity among predictors.
03
Model Fitting
Fit multiple regression models with increasing complexity. Apply stepwise selection and regularization to identify the best-fit specification.
04
Diagnostics
Check residual normality, homoscedasticity, and leverage. Test for multicollinearity using VIF and refine the model accordingly.
05
Prediction & Validation
Generate out-of-sample life expectancy predictions. Evaluate performance using RMSE and R² on a held-out test set.
